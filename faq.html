Frequently Asked Questions

Generative AI relies on sophisticated machine learning models called deep learning algorithms that simulate the learning and decision-making processes of the human brain. These models work by identifying and encoding patterns and relationships in huge amounts of data, and then use that information to understand users' natural language requests or questions and respond with relevant new content.

There are three phases for Generative AI to operate:
  1. Training: Create a foundation model for multiple GenAI applications.
  2. Tuning: Tailor the foundation model to a specific GenAI application.
  3. Generation, evaluation, and retuning: Assess output and continually improve quality and accuracy.

There are many GenAI tools used today. ChatGPT is the most widely used: an AI chatbot that can have human-like conversations, answer questions, and generate various types of content such as articles, code, emails, and creative text.

GenAI can produce what seems like diverse and original outputs. It can create content through capturing nuances in language, based on patterns which may not have been seen before in the training data. This helps to explore topics from a variety of viewpoints and opens up different perspectives.

Like other AI models, Generative AI models are trained on datasets, and if the data contains bias, it is reflected in the modelâ€™s output. Examples include text-to-image generators that amplify gender or racial stereotypes, or chatbots that demonstrate political bias.

GenAI detection software is currently unreliable and known to produce false positives, so open conversation and clear guidelines are recommended over detection tools.

When using third-party GenAI solutions, sensitive data might be exposed as requests often travel over the public internet. Best practices include using secure enterprise solutions that do not use customer data for model training.

The user or author is ultimately responsible for the accuracy, integrity, and originality of content produced using GenAI tools. Content should always be reviewed and validated by a human.
